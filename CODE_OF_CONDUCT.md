# Code of conduct

## Our standard

Project spaces should be safe, respectful, and useful for people with
different backgrounds and levels of experience. Examples of constructive
behavior include:

- communicating with empathy and technical clarity;
- giving specific, evidence-based feedback;
- accepting correction and taking responsibility for mistakes;
- respecting privacy, boundaries, and differing viewpoints;
- focusing on what is best for the project and its users.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, sexualized attention,
discriminatory language, personal attacks, deliberate intimidation, doxxing,
or publishing another person's private information without permission.
Sustained disruption, bad-faith argument, and retaliation for reporting a
concern are also unacceptable.

## Scope

This standard applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews,
discussions, project events, and other spaces where someone is representing
Apex Exec.

## Reporting and enforcement

For GitHub-hosted abuse, use
[GitHub's private abuse-reporting process](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse).
For a project-specific concern that is not a platform-abuse report, contact
the repository owner through the private contact method on the
[owner's GitHub profile](https://github.com/a-barwick). Do not publish
sensitive conduct reports in an issue.

Maintainers may edit or remove content and may warn, temporarily restrict, or
permanently exclude participants whose conduct is inconsistent with this
standard. Enforcement should consider context, impact, prior behavior,
acknowledgment, and repair while protecting reporters from retaliation.
